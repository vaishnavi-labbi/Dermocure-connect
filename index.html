About Skin Cancer

Skin cancer occurs when abnormal cells grow uncontrollably in the skin. It's the most common form of cancer worldwide.

Early Detection Saves Lives

When detected early, most skin cancers have high cure rates. Our system provides specialized tools for dermatologists to track patient cases, monitor suspicious lesions, and manage treatment plans.

We integrate advanced imaging with electronic health records to provide comprehensive care for patients at risk of or diagnosed with skin cancer.

Dermatologist Portal

Track patient cases, document lesions, and manage biopsy results through our specialized interface.

Dermoscopy Imaging

Store and compare serial images of suspicious lesions to monitor changes over time.

Follow-up Scheduling

Automated reminders for high-risk patients and those needing regular skin checks.

Pathology Integration

Direct access to biopsy results and pathology reports within patient records.

Types of Skin Cancer

The three most common types of skin cancer we diagnose and treat

Basal Cell Carcinoma

The most common type, usually appears as a waxy bump or flat lesion on sun-exposed areas.

More Info

Squamous Cell Carcinoma

Often appears as a firm red nodule or flat lesion with scaly crust, can metastasize if untreated.

More Info

Melanoma

The most serious form, can develop anywhere on the body. Early detection is critical.
